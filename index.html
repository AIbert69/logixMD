§ About Peptides

Targeted
biological
signaling.

Peptides are short chains of amino acids that act as signaling molecules in the body. Depending on their structure, peptides may interact with specific pathways involved in metabolism, tissue repair, cellular communication, skin health, and recovery.

Because peptide response varies by individual, peptides should be used responsibly with medical guidance, appropriate dosing, and a clear understanding of the product's intended use.

01

Cellular signaling

Peptides may help support communication between cells by interacting with specific receptors or biological pathways.

02

Targeted pathways

Different peptides are associated with different functions, including metabolic regulation, tissue support, mitochondrial signaling, and skin remodeling.

03

Personalized use

Peptide protocols should be tailored to the individual based on goals, health history, labs, medications, and clinician review.
